Welcome! Let me tell you a little bit about myself.

Hello! My name is Michael Goseco. I'm 36 year's old, from Riverside, California and I've been into programming ever since I was goofing around on my TI-83 back in high school. I hope to use this page as a demonstration of my skills as I continue to gain more knowledge in web and software development.

Early Life

As a child, I was very lucky in being exposed to computers at a young age. My father gave our family one of the first mass produced home computers: an old IBM x386. My brother and I first learned how to use a computer using DOS, then Windows 3.1, and so on. Despite growing up in a rural neighborhood, my elementary school had a computer lab ahead of its time, thanks to the efforts of a visionary teacher who woold later be my 3rd grade teacher, Mr. Heinz. From grades 1 to 6 my class would have computer lab time in a room filled with Apple IIs, and even in Mr. Heinz's class there was his own lab full of Commodore 64s that gave me even more experience with games and command lines. I'm certain I would be a much different person today if it weren't for these chance influences on my life.

Education

I studied Computer Science and C++ for 1 year at the University of California, Riverside in 2002. More recently however, I have been self learning many languages through the many resources on the internet including foundations in JavaScript, C#, and Java. Currently, I am a student at Codefellows receiving education in advanced web development and ASP.NET. I also have an A+ certificate and once had an EMT certificate in 2013 as part of the many interests I explored between my education in programming.
